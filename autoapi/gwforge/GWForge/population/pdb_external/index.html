gwforge.GWForge.population.pdb_external.power_law_dip_break_1d(mass, A, A2, NSmin, NSmax, BHmin, BHmax, UPPERmin, UPPERmax, n0, n1, n2, n3, n4, n5, alpha_1, alpha_2, alpha_dip, mu1, sig1, mix1, mu2, sig2, mix2, absolute_mmin, absolute_mmax)[source]¶

The one-dimensional mass distribution considered in Fishbach, Essick, Holz. Does Matter Matter? ApJ Lett 899, 1 (2020) : arXiv:2006.13178

\[\begin{split}p(m|\lambda) = n(m|\gamma_{\text{low}}, \gamma_{\text{high}}, A) \times l(m|m_{\text{max}}, \eta) \\ \times \begin{cases} & m^{\alpha_1} \text{ if } m < \gamma_{\text{low}} \\ & m^{\alpha_2} \text{ if } m > \gamma_{\text{low}} \\ & 0 \text{ otherwise } \end{cases}.\end{split}\]

where \(l(m|m_{\text{max}}, \eta)\) is the low pass filter with powerlaw \(\eta\) applied at mass \(m_{\text{max}}\), \(n(m|\gamma_{\text{low}}, \gamma_{\text{high}}, A)\) is the notch filter with depth \(A\) applied between \(\gamma_{\text{low}}\) and \(\gamma_{\text{high}}\), and \(\lambda\) is the subset of hyperparameters \(\{ \gamma_{\text{low}}, \gamma_{\text{high}}, A, \alpha_1, \alpha_2, m_{\text{min}}, m_{\ text{max}}\}\).

Parameters:

  • mass (array-like) – Mass to evaluate probability at (\(m\)).

  • alpha_1 (float) – Powerlaw exponent for compact objects below NSmax (\(\alpha_1\)).

  • alpha_2 (float) – Powerlaw exponent for compact objects above BHmin (\(\alpha_2\)).

  • alpha_dip (float) – Powerlaw exponent for compact objects between NSmax and BHmin (:math: alpha_d).

  • NSmin (float) – Minimum compact object mass (\(m_\min\)).

  • NSmax (float) – Mass at which the notch filter starts for the lower mass gap (\(\gamma_{low1}\)).

  • BHmin (float) – Mass at which the notch filter ends for the lower mass gap (\(\gamma_{high1}\)).

  • BHmax (float) – Maximum mass in the powerlaw distributed component (\(m_\max\)).

  • A (float) – depth of the dip between NSmax and BHmin (A).

  • UPPERmin (float) – Mass at which the notch filter starts for the upper mass gap (\(\gamma_{low2}\)).

  • UPPERmax (float) – Mass at which the notch filter ends for the upper mass gap (\(\gamma_{high2}\)).

  • mu1 (float) – Location of the upper peak where an overdensity of merging compact objects is observed (\(\mu_{peak1}\))..

  • sig1 (float) – Width of the upper peak where an overdensity of merging compact objects is observed (\(\sigma_{peak1}\))..

  • mix1 (float) – Mixing fraction of the first gaussian peak with the powerlaw + notches (:math: c_1)

  • mu2 (float) – Location of the lower peak where an overdensity of merging compact objects is observed (\(\mu_{peak2}\))..

  • sig2 (float) – Width of the lower peak where an overdensity of merging compact objects is observed (\(\mu_{peak2}\))..

  • mix2 (float) – Mixing fraction of the second gaussian peak with the powerlaw + notches (:math: c_2)

  • absolute_mmin (float) – The minimum limit for the truncated normal distribution.

  • absolute_mmax (float) – The maximum limit for the truncated normal distribution.

  • n{0 (float) – Exponents to set the sharpness of the low mass cutoff and high mass cutoff, respectively (\(\eta_i\)).

  • 5} (float) – Exponents to set the sharpness of the low mass cutoff and high mass cutoff, respectively (\(\eta_i\)).

  • n{1 (float) – Exponents to set the sharpness of the lower edge and upper edge of the lower mass gap, respectively (\(\eta_i\)).

  • 2} (float) – Exponents to set the sharpness of the lower edge and upper edge of the lower mass gap, respectively (\(\eta_i\)).

  • n{3 (float) – Exponents to set the sharpness of the lower edge and upper edge of the upper mass gap, respectively (\(\eta_i\)).

  • 4} (float) – Exponents to set the sharpness of the lower edge and upper edge of the upper mass gap, respectively (\(\eta_i\)).

Two-dimensional “Power Law + Dip + Break” Model.

Parameters:

  • dataset (dict) – Dictionary of numpy arrays for ‘mass_1’ (\(m_1\)) and ‘mass_ratio’ q (\(m_2=m_1*q\)).

  • alpha_1 (float) – Powerlaw exponent for compact object below break (\(lpha_1\)).

  • alpha_2 (float) – Powerlaw exponent for compact object above break (\(lpha_2\)).

  • NSmin (float) – Minimum compact object mass (\(m_\min\)).

  • NSmax (float) – Mass at which the notch filter starts (\(\gamma_{low}\))

  • BHmin (float) – Mass at which the notch filter ends (\(\gamma_{high}\)). Also, the mass value at which the power law exponent switches from alpha_1 to alpha_2.

  • BHmax (float) – Maximum mass in the powerlaw distributed component (\(m_\max\)).

  • n{0 (float) – Exponents to set the sharpness of the low mass cutoff, low edge of dip, high edge of dip, and high mass cutoff, respectively (\(\eta_i\)).

  • 1 (float) – Exponents to set the sharpness of the low mass cutoff, low edge of dip, high edge of dip, and high mass cutoff, respectively (\(\eta_i\)).

  • 2 (float) – Exponents to set the sharpness of the low mass cutoff, low edge of dip, high edge of dip, and high mass cutoff, respectively (\(\eta_i\)).

  • 3} (float) – Exponents to set the sharpness of the low mass cutoff, low edge of dip, high edge of dip, and high mass cutoff, respectively (\(\eta_i\)).

  • A (float) – depth of the dip between NSmax and BHmin (A).
